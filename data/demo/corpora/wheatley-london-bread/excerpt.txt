These dealers were privileged by law to receive thirteen batches for twelve, hence the expression "a baker's dozen."
Pannier Alley, leading from Newgate Street to Paternoster Row, was once the standing place for bakers with their bread panniers.
No maker of white bread was allowed to make tourte, nor a tourte baker to make white bread.
House bread was prepared by the bakers of household bread, while hostellers, by whom it was exclusively used, were forbidden to make it.
